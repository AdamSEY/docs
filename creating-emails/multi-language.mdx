## How to Generate Multi-Language

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Email">
    Write prompt in your primary language (usually English)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Languages">
    Click **Tools → Languages**
    
    Check all target languages:
    - ☑ Spanish
    - ☑ French
    - ☑ German
    etc.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate">
    Click **Generate**
    
    Migma creates separate versions for each language
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review Each Version">
    Switch between languages in tabs
    
    Each maintains your brand + cultural adaptation
  </Step>

  <Step title="Export All">
    Download all versions at once or individually
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Localization vs Translation

**Migma doesn't just translate—it localizes:**

| Translation | Localization (Migma) |
|-------------|---------------------|
| Word-for-word | Cultural adaptation |
| Same structure | Adjusted for locale |
| Direct conversion | Native-sounding |
| Formal only | Appropriate tone |
| Generic | Region-specific |

**Example:**

**English:** "Sign up for our newsletter to get 20% off your first purchase!"

**Spanish (Translation):** "¡Regístrese para nuestro boletín para obtener 20% de descuento en su primera compra!"

**Spanish (Migma Localization):** "¡Suscríbete a nuestro boletín y ahorra 20% en tu primera compra!"
- More natural "suscríbete" vs formal "regístrese"
- "ahorra" (save) more engaging than "obtener descuento"
- Maintains friendly tone

## Cultural Adaptation Features

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Date & Time Formats" icon="calendar">
    **Auto-adjusted per locale:**
    - US: MM/DD/YYYY → 12/31/2024
    - EU: DD/MM/YYYY → 31/12/2024
    - Japan: YYYY年MM月DD日 → 2024年12月31日
    
    **Time:**
    - US: 12-hour (2:30 PM)
    - EU: 24-hour (14:30)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Currency & Numbers" icon="dollar-sign">
    **Currency symbols:**
    - US: $99.99
    - EU: 99,99 €
    - UK: £99.99
    - Japan: ¥9,999
    
    **Number formatting:**
    - US: 1,000.50
    - EU: 1.000,50
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Names & Addresses" icon="address-card">
    **Name order:**
    - Western: First Last
    - Eastern: Last First
    
    **Address format** adjusted per country standards
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Tone & Formality" icon="comments">
    **Adapts to cultural norms:**
    - German: More formal
    - Spanish: Informal "tú" vs formal "usted"
    - Japanese: Appropriate honorifics

## Best Practices

<Check>**Start with English** for best results, then generate other languages</Check>
